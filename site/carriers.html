Straightforward coordination

Peer is preparing to make load handoffs clear.

Carriers should know the freight, schedule, locations, requirements, and operating contact before committing. When new information appears, Peer will keep the current plan and next action clear.

02

04

Respect for the work

A carrier should not have to decode the load or chase the broker.

Peer is preparing an operating model that treats drivers and dispatchers as partners in the same shipment. Good coordination means accurate information, direct answers, realistic expectations, and a clear response when a facility or schedule creates a problem.

  • Pickup and delivery instructions will be confirmed before the handoff.
  • Peer will communicate material changes when they become known.
  • Shipment specific safety and compliance requirements will stay explicit.
  • Peer will not describe freight as ready before the operating requirements are met.

Carrier qualification

Peer will verify that the carrier, equipment, and shipment belong together.

Before live operations begin, Peer is preparing a carrier qualification process that reviews authority, insurance, identity, safety information, equipment fit, and shipment specific requirements. Specialized freight will require additional checks.

Identity and authority will be verified.

Peer will confirm that the operating entity and the people communicating about the load match reliable records.

Insurance and equipment will be reviewed.

Coverage, equipment type, and operating fit will be checked against the shipment before tender.

Special requirements will stay specific.

Hazmat, tanker, temperature controlled, and open deck freight will require the endorsements, procedures, and equipment relevant to that shipment.

Carrier questions

Peer is not onboarding carriers before launch.

Can I submit my carrier information now?

No. Peer is not collecting carrier packets or soliciting capacity while operating authority is pending. The website will change when onboarding opens for actual launch freight.

What will Peer require from carriers?

Peer is preparing to verify operating authority, identity, insurance, safety information, equipment, and shipment specific requirements. The exact process will be published before onboarding opens.

Will Peer work with specialized carriers?

Peer is preparing for refrigerated, flatbed, tanker, and hazmat freight as well as dry van. Those loads will require qualified carriers, compatible equipment, and the insurance, endorsements, and procedures relevant to the shipment.

How will Peer communicate during a load?

Peer is designing the service around a responsive operating contact, confirmed instructions, practical status updates, and direct exception coordination from dispatch through delivery.
