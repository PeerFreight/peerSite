A connected service

Peer is preparing to manage the work between the tender and the proof of delivery.

That means treating coverage, dispatch, tracking, appointments, exceptions, and documents as one responsibility instead of a series of disconnected handoffs.

  1. 01

    We will confirm the load before it moves.

    Peer will review the shipment details, equipment, schedule, locations, and requirements before coordinating a carrier.

  2. 02

    We will keep the shipment visible in transit.

    Peer will stay close to pickup, transit, appointments, and delivery, then share the information your team needs to act.

  3. 03

    We will keep exceptions moving toward a clear next step.

    When timing, equipment, or instructions change, Peer will coordinate the response until the next action and owner are clear.

  4. 04

    We will close the operational loop.

    Peer will follow through on delivery status and documents so loose ends do not become another queue for your team.

Communication that helps

Your team should get an answer, the current status, and the next action.

Peer is preparing to make updates useful instead of noisy. Routine movement should be easy to understand, and an exception should include who is working it and what happens next.

  • Pickup and appointment status will be communicated clearly.
  • Material changes will be surfaced without waiting for a chase.
  • Carrier and facility coordination will stay with Peer.
  • Delivery and document follow through will have a clear owner.

Exception ownership

When the plan changes, the work should not fall back on your team.

Peer is preparing operating playbooks for late trucks, missed appointments, equipment mismatches, rejected freight, temperature concerns, documentation gaps, and delivery changes. Each situation still requires shipment specific judgment, but every exception should have a clear owner.

Peer will get the facts first.

We will confirm what changed, who is affected, and which decisions are time sensitive.

Peer will coordinate both sides.

We will keep the shipper, carrier, and facility aligned around the current plan.

Peer will follow the issue through.

We will stay with the exception until the next handoff is clear and documented.

Shipper questions

The launch boundary is intentionally clear.

Can I send Peer a load to quote now?

No. Peer is not accepting load details or quoting live freight while operating authority is pending. The website will change when Peer is ready to begin shipper conversations.

Where will Peer offer coverage?

Peer is preparing to operate as a U.S. freight brokerage, but active lanes have not been finalized. Coverage will depend on launch scope, carrier availability, equipment, commodity, and operating requirements.

Will Peer support specialized freight?

Peer is preparing for refrigerated, flatbed, tanker, and hazmat freight as well as dry van. Specialized freight will only be supported when shipment requirements, insurance, carrier qualifications, and compliance controls are satisfied.

What will happen when a load has a problem?

Peer is designing the service so the team coordinating the shipment also owns the response. We will confirm the facts, coordinate the affected parties, communicate the next step, and stay responsible until the next action and owner are clear.
